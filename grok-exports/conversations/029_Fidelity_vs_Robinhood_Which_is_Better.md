## Message 2 - ASSISTANT

**Fidelity** and **Robinhood** are both popular brokerage platforms with **commission-free** stock and ETF trading, but they cater to different types of investors. There's no universal "better" one—it depends on your goals, experience level, and what you plan to trade.

### Key Comparison (as of early 2026)

| Category              | Fidelity                                                                 | Robinhood                                                              |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Best for**          | Long-term investors, beginners needing education/research, retirement planning, diversified portfolios | Mobile-first users, active traders, options/crypto enthusiasts, simple low-cost trading |
| **Investment Options**| Stocks, ETFs, mutual funds (including zero-expense-ratio index funds), options, bonds/CDs, fractional shares, limited crypto (BTC/ETH) | Stocks, ETFs, options, crypto (wider selection), futures, fractional shares. No mutual funds, bonds, or CDs |
| **Fees & Commissions**| $0 stocks/ETFs<br>$0.65 per options contract<br>Higher margin rates (~11-13%) | $0 stocks/ETFs/options<br>Lower margin rates (~5-6% with Gold subscription)<br>Gold: $5/month for perks |
| **IRA/Retirement**    | Wide range (Traditional, Roth, etc.), strong tools, no match            | Traditional/Roth with 1% match (3% with Gold), but limited investments (no mutual funds) |
| **Platform & Tools**  | Multiple platforms (mobile, desktop Active Trader Pro), advanced charting, extensive research/education | Sleek mobile app, simple interface, basic tools |
| **Customer Service**  | Excellent (phone, chat, branches), highly rated                          | Improved (24/7 support), but historically weaker |
| **Ratings (2025 reviews)** | Often top-rated overall (e.g., #1 by Investopedia, StockBrokers.com high scores) | Strong for simplicity/mobile, but lower for features/depth |

### Which One Should You Choose?
- **Choose Fidelity** if: You're building a long-term portfolio, want mutual funds (especially low-cost index funds), need research/tools/education, or plan for retirement with more options. It's frequently ranked as the best overall broker and surprisingly strong for beginners despite its depth (e.g., NerdWallet's Best for Beginners award multiple years).
- **Choose Robinhood** if: You want a super-simple app for trading stocks/options/crypto, zero options fees, lower margin costs, or the IRA match bonus. It's ideal for casual/mobile trading but lacks depth for serious diversification.

Many experts (from NerdWallet, Investopedia, StockBrokers.com) consider **Fidelity** the stronger all-around choice for most investors due to its broader offerings and reliability. If you're just starting with basic stock/ETF buying, either works great—try both apps to see the interface fit.
