### 3. Psychological and Psychiatric Profiles of Serial Offenders
#### 3.1 Paraphilic Disorders and Sexual Deviation

#### 3.1.1 The Jeffrey Dahmer Case: Necrophilia and Polymorphic Paraphilia
Jeffrey Dahmer represents one of the most extensively documented cases of paraphilic serial homicide. Between 1978 and 1991, Dahmer murdered 17 young men and boys, engaging in acts of necrophilia, dismemberment, and cannibalism. His case provides critical insight into the intersection of multiple psychiatric disorders. Forensic psychiatrists diagnosed Dahmer with a constellation of conditions: necrophilia (paraphilic disorder characterized by sexual attraction to corpses), borderline personality disorder (marked by fear of abandonment and emotional instability), schizotypal personality disorder, alcohol dependence, and a psychotic disorder (Giannetakis, 2017; Silva et al., 2002). Defense expert Dr. Fred Berlin testified that Dahmer suffered from an irresistible compulsion—describing his affliction as a "cancer of the mind" and a "broken mind" that rendered him unable to conform his conduct (Psychiatric Testimony, Criminal Profiling Database). The academic literature describes Dahmer's psychopathology as "polymorphic paraphilic spectrum disorder," with libido focused on visceral matter, organs, and bones due to developmental failures in interpersonal attachment (Giannetakis, 2017). His psychological profile included sexual sadism, obsessive-compulsive traits, and what forensic experts termed "compulsive need for control" rather than purely sadistic motivation. Unlike classic psychopaths who kill for power and pleasure, Dahmer's crimes were driven by profound emotional needs—specifically, terror of abandonment and desperate attempts to create compliant companions who would never leave him.

#### 3.1.2 Necrophilia: Prevalence and Typology
Necrophilia, defined as sexual attraction to or acts with corpses, represents one of the most extreme paraphilias. Geofence warrants are a type of court-authorized search order issued to technology companies (primarily Google, due to its vast Android location data via Google Maps and other services) requiring them to disclose information about all mobile devices that were present within a specified geographic boundary ("geofence") during a particular time period.
