Key US Uprisings from Poverty and Government Neglect

Harlem Riot (1935, NYC): Great Depression-era Black unemployment hit 50%; police brutality rumor sparked targeting of exploitative businesses. Result: 3 deaths, hundreds injured, $2M damage (~$45M today).
Watts Riots (1965, LA): 30% unemployment, 50% below poverty line in Black areas; police arrest ignited 6 days of rage over jobs/housing. Outcome: 34 deaths, 1,000+ injuries, $40M damage (~$400M today). McCone Commission blamed "poverty and despair."
Newark Riots (1967, NJ): 40% Black poverty, double national unemployment; cab driver beating led to 5 days protesting discrimination. Result: 26 deaths, 700+ injuries, $10M damage. Part of 150+ "long, hot summer" events.
Detroit Riot (1967, MI): 25% Black unemployment in inner cities; bar raid escalated to deadliest unrest, hitting inequality symbols. Outcome: 43 deaths, 1,189 injuries, 7,200 arrests, $50M damage. Kerner tied to "inner-city poverty."
LA Riots (1992): 30% poverty, 45% youth unemployment in South Central; Rodney King verdict fueled 6 days over neglect. Result: 63 deaths, 2,383 injuries, $1B damage—US costliest then.
Others: 1966 Hunters Point (SF, police in poor Black areas); 1969 Greensboro (NC, student anti-poverty); 2014 Ferguson (MO, 21% poverty, economic disparities).
