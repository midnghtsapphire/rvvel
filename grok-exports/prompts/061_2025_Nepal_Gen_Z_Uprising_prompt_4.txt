Harlem Riot (1935, New York City): Sparked by rumors of police brutality amid the Great Depression, where Black unemployment reached 50% and poverty was rampant. Newark Riots (1967, New Jersey): In a city where Black poverty was at 40% and unemployment double the national rate, a police beating of a Black cab driver ignited five days of unrest. Detroit Riot (1967, Michigan): Amid 25% Black unemployment and concentrated poverty in inner-city areas, a police raid on an unlicensed bar led to the deadliest uprising of the era. The Kerner Commission explicitly blamed "inner-city poverty and despair."

Los Angeles Riots (1992): Following the acquittal of officers in the Rodney King beating, in South Central LA where poverty affected 30% of residents and youth unemployment was 45%, six days of rioting ensued.
