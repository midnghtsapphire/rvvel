 "3 3 3 rule" in marketing refers to different strategies focused on simplification and clarity. One common interpretation involves focusing on three key messages, three core audience segments, and three marketing channels. Another interpretation focuses on the three components of content: educational, inspirational, and entertaining; three distribution channels: owned, earned, and paid; and three audience engagement stages: awareness, consideration, and acquisition. 
Strategy 1: Simplify and focus 
This approach helps clarify and streamline a marketing strategy by focusing on three key areas: 
Three key messages: Define the three most important things you want to communicate about your brand, product, or service.
Three core audiences: Identify the three most important customer segments to target with your messaging.
Three marketing channels: Concentrate your efforts on the three channels where your core audience spends the most time.
Strategy 2: Balance content, channels, and engagement 
This interpretation is a framework for creating a balanced and effective content strategy: 
Three types of content: Create a mix of educational, inspirational, and entertaining content to engage your audience on multiple levels.
Three key distribution channels: Utilize a mix of owned media (your own channels), earned media (publicity, reviews), and paid media (advertising) to maximize reach.
Three audience engagement stages: Tailor your content to guide your audience through the buyer's journey, addressing their needs at the awareness, consideration, and acquisition stages.
