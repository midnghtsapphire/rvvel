# Skill: Historic Media Expert  
## ID: historic_media_expert  
## Category: History & Culture Part 2  
## Version: 1.0  
## Created: 2026-02-15  

## Description  
The Historic Media Expert skill enables Audrey Evans to explore, analyze, and interpret historic media archives, including newspapers, microfilm collections, and digitized press materials. It provides access to global repositories like the Library of Congress’s Chronicling America, the British Newspaper Archive, and the Bibliothèque nationale de France. This skill is essential for Audrey’s historical research, allowing her to track media evolution from colonial broadsheets to modern journalism, analyze propaganda techniques, and understand the history of press freedom. It also supports her work in media preservation and digitization projects, aligning with her expertise in database programming and archival systems.  

## Capabilities  
1. Access and search millions of digitized newspaper pages using the Chronicling America API ([chroniclingamerica.loc.gov](https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/about/api/)).  
2. Analyze historic media trends, including yellow journalism and propaganda techniques.  
3. Explore microfilm collections and digitization projects from the National Archives (NARA) and Smithsonian.  
4. Study the evolution of press freedom and censorship across different eras.  
5. Access uncensored historic newspapers from colonial broadsheets to modern media.  
6. Learn media preservation techniques, including digitization best practices.  
7. Analyze global media archives, such as the British Library and Bibliothèque nationale de France.  
8. Cross-reference historical events with contemporaneous media coverage.  
9. Study the impact of media on public opinion and political movements.  
10. Access Newspapers.com’s free resources for historic newspaper research.  
11. Explore digitization projects funded by the National Endowment for the Humanities ([neh.gov](https://www.neh.gov/)).  
12. Analyze the role of media in shaping cultural and societal norms.  
13. Study the history of investigative journalism and its impact on society.  
14. Access the British Newspaper Archive for UK-centric historic media.  
15. Learn about the technical specifications for digitizing historic newspapers ([libraryofcongress.github.io](https://libraryofcongress.github.io/data-exploration/loc.gov%20JSON%20API/Chronicling_America/README.html)).

## Data Sources & FOSS Tools  
- Chronicling America API: Access millions of digitized newspaper pages ([chroniclingamerica.loc.gov](https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/about/api/)).  
- British Newspaper Archive: Explore UK-centric historic newspapers ([britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk](https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/)).  
- National Archives (NARA): Access microfilm collections and digitization projects ([archives.gov](https://www.archives.gov/)).  
- Newspapers.com: Free resources for historic newspaper research ([newspapers.com](https://www.newspapers.com/)).

## Limitations  
- Cannot access paywalled content from premium archives.  
- Limited to publicly available digitized collections.  
- May encounter OCR errors in digitized text ([chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/ocr](https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/ocr/)).  
- Does not include real-time updates on new digitization projects.  
- Limited to media archives with open APIs or public access.  
- Requires internet access for API-based queries.

## Description  
The Bible Expert skill equips Audrey Evans with the ability to analyze biblical texts from a Nigerian Christian perspective, explore uncensored Bible versions, and study original languages like Hebrew, Aramaic, and Koine Greek. It includes textual criticism, analysis of mistranslations, and exploration of apocryphal texts like the Dead Sea Scrolls and Gnostic Gospels. This skill supports Audrey’s historical research by providing insights into biblical archaeology, manuscript traditions, and hermeneutics. It also aligns with her interest in cultural history and her Nigerian heritage.

1. Study the Nigerian perspective on Christianity and Bible interpretation.

3. Explore original languages: Hebrew (Tanakh), Aramaic (Daniel, Ezra), and Koine Greek (New Testament).  
4. Identify and critique mistranslations, such as KJV errors and the Comma Johanneum.  
5. Study the Dead Sea Scrolls and their impact on biblical scholarship.  
6. Explore apocryphal and Deuterocanonical books.  
7. Analyze the Gnostic Gospels and their theological implications.  
8. Study the Ethiopian Bible, which includes 81 books.  
9. Apply textual criticism to biblical manuscripts.  
10. Compare manuscript traditions: Masoretic, Septuagint, and Vulgate.  
11. Explore biblical archaeology and its findings.  
12. Study hermeneutics and interpretive methods.  
13. Analyze the historical context of biblical texts.  
14. Explore the impact of Bible translations on global Christianity.  
15. Study the role of the Bible in cultural and societal development.
